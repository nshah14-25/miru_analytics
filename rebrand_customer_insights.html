Key insights

Positive

Revenue grew despite fewer customers

Total sales are up +1.2% (+$3,499) even with 52 fewer customers post-rebrand. Each customer is now spending more — overall revenue per customer improved from $138.64 to $143.84 (+$5.20). The rebrand is lifting basket value.

Positive

New customers are spending significantly more

New customer AOV jumped from $141.51 to $146.17 (+3.3%) and revenue per new customer went from $141.83 to $151.22 (+$9.39). The rebrand is attracting new customers with higher purchase intent and willingness to spend.

Watch

Returning customers spending less per order

AOV for returning customers dropped from $125.46 to $121.78 (−2.9%). Loyal customers may be hesitating post-rebrand. A targeted re-engagement campaign for this segment is worth prioritising now.

Watch

Customer volume slightly down across both segments

Both new (−2.5%) and returning (−2.4%) customer counts declined by a similar margin — suggesting a broader traffic or conversion dip rather than a loyalty problem specifically. Likely a short-term post-rebrand disruption.

Signal

New : returning ratio held perfectly stable

The ratio of new to returning customers was exactly 1.42:1 both before and after the rebrand. The rebrand hasn't disproportionately hurt either segment — the mix of your customer base is fully intact.

Signal

New customers now drive 61.6% of revenue

Up from 60% pre-rebrand. Combined with higher AOV from new buyers, the new brand positioning is resonating with first-time purchasers — the exact audience you need to grow CLTV from via loyalty programmes.
